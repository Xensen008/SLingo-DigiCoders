What is Sign Language?

Sign languages are languages that use the visual-manual modality to convey meaning, instead of spoken words.Wherever communities of deaf people exist, sign languages have developed as useful means of communication and form the core of local deaf cultures. Signing is used primarily by the deaf and hard of hearing. Each country generally has its own native sign language; some have more than one.
